EXCHANGE IN AUSTRALIA: HOSPITALITY AND CULTURAL DIVERSITY.

Studying abroad in Australia has been very successful among Brazilians for several reasons: the tropical climate is very similar to that of some Brazilian regions, the people are very hospitable, the beaches favor surfing and the Australian dollar is cheaper than the American.

More than that, the country is a much sought after destination for its excellent quality of life, security and an enviable educational system, with prices lower than in other developed countries.

The country has several options of cities along the coast, each with its peculiarities. All of them are clean, organized and have a quality of life that earned Australia second place in the Human Development Index (HDI) in 2012, second only to Norway.

In addition to studying English in Australia, during your stay you can get to know and experience the enormous cultural and ethnic diversity, not to mention the variety of landscapes: those who venture into the interior of the country can come across both ski resorts high in the snowy mountains as with arid deserts.

With so many attractions, Australia is one of the two countries that receives the most young people to study English. Thanks to its beaches and beautiful landscapes, it is the ideal place to combine studies with travel and sports activities, favoring or learning.

Study IT in Australia and become a coveted professional in the market

The Information Technology professional is coveted around the world, and this is no different in Australia. Therefore, exchange students have increasingly sought and invested in programs in the area.

According to data released by the Australian government, the number of people working with IT has grown strongly in recent times in the country, and the forecast for the next five years is even more optimistic: from 65,800 in 2018 to 77,900 by 2023. Salaries are even more attractive. Support technicians earn an average of AUD 1,498 per week and managers, AUD 2,766 per week.

And to meet the strong demand of the job market, schools and universities invest in cutting-edge courses that serve both new students and those who already have some qualification.
